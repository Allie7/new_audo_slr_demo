research_direction: Randomised controlled trials (RCTs) assessing the efficacy of vitamin A in preventing measles-induced blind* and related ocular conditions such as xerosis*, xerophthalmia, and other ocular abnormalities. The studies should exclude participants with prior clinical features of vitamin A deficiency or malnutrition, as well as those with adverse events or vitamin A toxicity.

filter_requirements: Search methods: CENTRAL 2015, Issue 11, MEDLINE (1950 to December week 3, 2015), Embase (1974 to December 2015) and LILACS (1985 to December 2015). Selection criteria: Randomised controlled trials (RCTs) assessing vitamin A's efficacy in preventing measles-induced blind* or related ocular conditions such as xerosis*, xerophthalmia, night blind症, conjunctival xerosis, Bitot's spot, corneal xerosis, xerophthalmia, corneal ulceration, corneal scars, serum retinol level, nutritional status, adverse events, vitamin A toxicity, or other clinical vitamin A deficiencies. Excluded studies with participants having clinical vitamin A deficiency, ocular abnormalities unrelated to vitamin A deficiency, night blind症, conjunctival xerosis, Bitot's spot, corneal ulceration, corneal scars, serum retinol level, nutritional status, adverse events, vitamin A toxicity, or other clinical vitamin A deficiencies.
