research_direction: Early vs Late Tracheotomy for Prevention of Pneumonia  
filter_requirements: Included all randomized (RCTs) and quasi‐randomized controlled trials (QRCTs) published in any language. We approached the principal authors for additional information when necessary. Critically ill patients (for whom death is possible or imminent). Patients expected to be on prolonged mechanical ventilation. Adults (≥ 18 years). Prolonged mechanical ventilation defined as ventilation provided for 24 hours to 21 consecutive days, six or more hours per day (Divo 2010; Shirzad 2010). Anatomical anomalies of the neck that would impair the tracheostomy procedure. Previous tracheostomy. Coagulation disturbances (e.g., thrombocytopenia). Soft tissue infection of the neck. Rejection based on late tracheostomy performed between 6 and 10 days after intubation.  
other_info: We considered all outcome measures reported in the primary studies. For each outcome, we accepted the definition used by the study authors. We discussed limitations such as use of non‐validated instruments for evaluation or divergence of definitions. Mortality (time to mortality or frequency of deaths at any time point: in hospital, ICU, or after discharge). Duration of artificial ventilation. Length of stay in ICU (or frequency of tracheostomy at any time point). Ventilator‐associated pneumonia at any time point. Laryngotracheal lesions at any time point (in epiglottis, vocal cord, larynx; subglottic ulceration and inflammation; stenosis).  
other_info: Inclusion based on critical illness, prolonged mechanical ventilation, age ≥18 years, neck anomalies like tracheostomy impairment, previous tracheostomy, coagulation disturbances, soft tissue infections, and late tracheostomy performed between 6 and 10 days after intubation.  

This research direction specifically focuses on comparing early versus late tracheotomy procedures for their potential benefits in preventing pneumonia among mechanically ventilated adult ICU patients. The field is classified under medicine, specifically surgical/anaesthesia care.
