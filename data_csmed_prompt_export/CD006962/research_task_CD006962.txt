The review evaluates the effectiveness and safety of two-dimensional (US) and Doppler ultrasound (USD)-guided techniques compared to landmark-guided methods for insertion into internal jugular, subclavian, or femoral veins in adults and children. The study found that US reduced complications by 71%, decreased arterial punctures by 72%, increased success rates modestly, decreased the number of attempts needed, increased first-try success by 57%, reduced hematomas by 73%, and decreased cannulation time by 30.52 seconds. Doppler US showed a 58% increase in first-try success but no significant differences for other outcomes.
