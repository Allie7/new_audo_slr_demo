research_direction: Assessment of perioperative beta‐adrenergic blockade effects on cardiac complications in non‐cardiac vascular surgery  
filter_requirements: Inclusion criteria: prospective, randomised controlled trials of perioperative beta‐adrenergic blockade. Adults over 18 years of age undergoing non‐cardiac vascular surgery. Intervention: perioperative beta‐adrenergic blockers of any dose, titration, duration and mode of administration. Control: placebo or no treatment. All‐cause mortality. Cardiovascular mortality. 30‐day postoperative non‐fatal and fatal myocardial infarction is excluded from this review. Arrhythmia (any variation from the normal rhythm of the heart beat) requiring treatment. Heart failure. Vascular patency/graft occlusion. Stroke. Composite 30‐day cardiovascular outcomes. Intra‐operative bradycardia. Intra‐operative hypotension. Re‐hospitalisation at 30 days. Exclusion criteria: any other interventions, e.g., antiplatelets, diuretics, or calcium channel blockers.  
other_info: The meta‐analysis included two studies comparing perioperative beta‐adrenergic blockade with placebo on cardiovascular outcomes in people undergoing major non‐cardiac vascular surgery. The overall quality of studies was good, but one study lacked random sequence generation or allocation concealment techniques, and the other did not report outcome assessor blinding and was possibly underpowered. There was no evidence that perioperative beta‐adrenergic blockade reduced all‐cause mortality, cardiovascular mortality, non‐fatal myocardial infarction, arrhythmia, heart failure, stroke, composite cardiovascular events, or re‐hospitalisation at 30 days. However, there was strong evidence of increased intra‐operative bradycardia and hypotension. Additionally, DECREASE studies' data cannot be relied upon due to scientific misconduct involving the principal investigator, Don Poldermans, who was dismissed by Erasmus University for alleged misconduct including failing to obtain written patient consent and negligent data collection.
