research_direction: 精神分裂症的维持治疗  
other_info: The review includes approximately 10 studies with a total sample size of around 300 participants, focusing on various types of mental disorders including neurodegenerative diseases. Chlorpromazine reduced relapse rates compared to placebo but showed heterogeneous data, indicating varying effects across studies. It provided global improvement in symptoms and functioning, though adverse effects such as sedation, movement disorders, and weight gain were significant. The review prioritizes randomised controlled trials with double-blind methods where sensitivity analysis was conducted. If such studies significantly influenced the results, they were retained; otherwise, quasi-randomised studies were excluded. Additionally, there is evidence that 5-hydroxytryptamine increases platelet aggregation responses in patients taking chlorpromazine, though this finding comes from a non-randomised, case-control study design with potential limitations due to its observational nature. Participants were excluded if they had dementia or movement disorders, rather than schizophrenia.
