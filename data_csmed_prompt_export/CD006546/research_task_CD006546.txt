research_direction: Controlled experimental and quasi-experimental studies comparing children in kinship care with those in foster care, focusing on outcomes such as behaviour development, mental health, placement stability, permanency, educational attainment, family relations, service utilisation, re-abuse, and quality of care. Specific outcome indicators include behaviour problems, adaptive behaviours, delinquency; psychiatric illnesses, psychopathological conditions, well-being; number of placements, re-entry, length of stay; reunification, adoption, guardianship; graduation, grades, test scores, attendance, academic success; problem-solving, tolerance, commitment, conflicts, emotional availability, home environment; mental health services, foster support groups, family therapy, developmental services, and physician services. Quality of care assessment involves evaluating the effectiveness of kinship and foster family care in providing supportive environments for children maltreated.
