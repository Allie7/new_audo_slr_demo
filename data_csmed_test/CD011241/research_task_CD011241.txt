research_direction: Diabetic Neuropathy Treatment  
filter_requirements: Included studies were randomised controlled trials (RCTs) with double-blind assessment of participant outcomes following two weeks of treatment or longer, with a focus on studies lasting eight weeks or more. Time frame was at least two weeks, excluding non-randomised trials and case reports. Analysis focused on chronic pain patients, particularly those with painful diabetic neuropathy, showing moderate to substantial relief in pain levels measured by NRS or VAS scales. Primary outcomes included at least 30% pain relief (moderate benefit) or at least 50% pain relief (substantial benefit), as well as significant improvement on the Pain Global Impact of Chronic Illness Scale (PGIC). Adverse events such as somnolence, dizziness, and withdrawal symptoms due to adverse effects were noted. The study population included patients with chronic pain conditions such as spinal cord injury, poststroke pain, cancer-related neuropathy, HIV neuropathy, and complex regional pain syndrome (CRPS), with a focus on diabetes-related neuropathies like postherpetic neuralgia and phantom limb pain.
