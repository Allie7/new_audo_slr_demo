research_direction: Cognitive Bias Modification Interventions (CBMI) for Depression and Quality of Life in Dementia Patients, as well as Increasing Enjoyable Activities to Treat Depression in Nursing Home Residents with Dementia: A Pilot Study  
other_info: Cognitive Bias Modification Interventions (CBMI) are psychological interventions designed to reduce cognitive distortions, enhance critical thinking, and improve emotional regulation. These interventions focus on training participants' attentional resources to minimize exposure to negative stimuli and promote adaptive thinking patterns. Types of CBMI include cognitive behavioural therapy (CBT), cognitive restructuring, and systematic debriefing tailored to the cognitive profile of dementia patients. The control conditions typically involve no treatment or comparison groups receiving non-specific psychosocial activities. Outcome measures often include validated scales such as the Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (HDRS), Rating Anxiety in Dementia Scale (RADS), and the Pressure Scales Questionnaire for Items related to mental health (PSQI). These tools assess changes in depressive symptoms, anxiety levels, and quality of life indicators like daily living skills and caregiver burden. Research has shown that CBMI can be effective in reducing depression and improving overall well-being in dementia patients when implemented as structured, facilitated interventions within clinical or community settings. Additionally, a pilot study aims to investigate the impact of increasing enjoyable activities on depression treatment for elderly residents with dementia, with potential implications for future therapeutic strategies.
