research_direction: Artemisinin-based Combination Therapy (ACT) for Treating uncomplicated Plasmodium falciparum and Plasmodium vivax malaria; Dose-Optimization Studies of Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine  
filter_requirements: Cohort study; Randomized controlled trial (RCT); inclusion criteria: adult and child (including pregnant women and infants) with uncomplicated P. falciparum or P. vivax malaria, no complications; treatment with DHA-P or ACT three times; follow-up at day 28, 42, and 63, with PCR-adjusted and unadjusted analysis; exclusion criteria: death, severe adverse events (e.g., hospitalization or discontinuation of treatment), laboratoryhematologic and biochemical adverse reactions (e.g., leukopenia, hepatotoxicity), early nausea, and other adverse events.  
other_info: DHA-P versus artemether-lumefantrine: superior in Africa at preventing further parasitaemia, with RR 0.34 (95% CI 0.30-0.39) for PCR-unadjusted treatment failure across nine trials; no differences shown in Asia and Oceania at day 28 or day 63. DHA-P versus artesunate plus mefloquine: as effective in Asia with PCR-unadjusted treatment failure RR 0.41 (95% CI 0.21-0.80); fewer recurrent parasitaemia with artesunate plus mefloquine in South America. Trials conducted in southern China with participants aged 12-59 years old; A randomized trial of artemether-lumefantrine and dihydroartemisinin-piperaquine in the treatment of uncomplicated malaria among children in western Kenya should be included as part of this study. Randomized, controlled Dose-Optimization Studies of Dihydroartemisinin-Piperaquine for the Treatment of Uncomplicated Multidrug-Resistant Falciparum Malaria in Thailand: Participants are 18-59 years old and enrolled in a randomized trial.
