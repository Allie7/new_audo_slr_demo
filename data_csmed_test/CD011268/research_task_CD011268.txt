research_direction: Prevention of winter-type Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) using selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), serotonin and norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors (SNRIs), and other medications, as well as light therapy. The study included randomised controlled trials (RCTs; including cross-over studies and cluster-randomised trials) of SSRIs such as citalopram, escitalopram, fluoxetine, fluvoxamine, paroxetine, and sertraline for prevention of winter-type SAD. Non-RCT studies such as non-randomised trials, prospective cohort studies or case-control studies were also included. The inclusion criteria focused on participants with winter-type SAD and common comorbidities like diabetes or cardiovascular disease who were not the cause of their depressive episode. Exclusion criteria included participants with depressive disorder due to another medical condition and mixed populations without adequate stratification.

Additionally, a systematic review on the efficacy and safety of light therapy for SAD prevention was conducted, excluding studies that did not investigate the efficacy nor safety of selective gamma sodium adenosine (SGA) medications as preventive treatment.
