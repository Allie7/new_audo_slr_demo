research_direction: 不同代际抗ipsychotic药物的效果与安全性对比分析  
Search conducted from October 2011 to July 2013 using Cochrane Schizophrenia Group’s Trials Register and various databases, as well as in the past two years. Included all randomized controlled trials (RCTs) comparing oral haloperidol with other first‐generation antipsychotic drugs, excluding low‐potency ones, and all RCTs comparing third-generation antipsychotics with other drugs. Quality of studies was assessed using the Cochrane Collaboration's 'Risk of bias' tool. The systematic review found no clear evidence of a difference in primary outcomes between haloperidol and other first‐generation antipsychotics in short‐term trials (medium effect size), with medium‐term trials showing haloperidol to be less effective. Haloperidol showed fewer akathisia effects in the medium term. Research utilized GRADE method for evidence quality assessment and constructed summary tables to present efficacy, safety, and economic impact of different interventions. Additionally, cost-benefit analyses were conducted for first and second-generation antipsychotics, with a focus on the comparative effectiveness and long-term outcomes of haloperidol in medium-term trials; third-generation antipsychotics were also compared with second-generation drugs to assess their relative effectiveness and long-term safety profiles.
