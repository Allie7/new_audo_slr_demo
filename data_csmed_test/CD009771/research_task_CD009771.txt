research_direction: Protocolized Sedation vs Usual Care in Pediatric Patients Mechanically Ventilated for Acute Respiratory Failure  
filter_requirements: Including non-RCT studies  
other_info: We included observational studies, case reports, and expert opinions published within the last two years. The target intervention was the implementation of a protocol-based sedation management algorithm. The comparison group included usual care (sedation management as per local practice without any specific sedation strategies). The primary outcome measure was the duration of mechanical ventilation in hours for the entire duration of the first ICU stay for each patient. Secondary outcomes included ICU length of stay, hospital mortality, total dose of sedation administered (mg), adverse events within ICU (self-extubation or reintubation), incidence of delirium in ICU, and psychological recovery after hospital discharge using validated measures. Memory function, cognitive recovery, quality of life, and tracheostomy status were also assessed post-discharge. Where a study reported both ICU and hospital outcomes, the respective data points were included accordingly.
