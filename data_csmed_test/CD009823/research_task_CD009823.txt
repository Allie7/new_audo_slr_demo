research_direction: Interventions aimed at promoting reintegration and reducing harmful behaviour and lifestyles among street-connected children and young people. Objectives To evaluate and summarise the effectiveness of interventions for street-connected children and young people that aim to: promote inclusion and reintegration; increase literacy and numeracy; facilitate access to education and employment; promote mental health, including self-esteem; reduce harms associated with early sexual activity and substance misuse. Other information includes targeting outcomes such as reducing risky sexual activity, substance use, increasing education and employment opportunities, providing shelter support, and enhancing mental health outcomes among children aged 15 to 24 years old living or working in urban environments. Search methods included searching various bibliographic databases from inception to 2012 and non-governmental websites. Selection criteria focused on studies using comparison group study design, randomised controlled trials (RCTs), clinical controlled trials (CCTs), controlled before-and-after trials (CBAs) and quasi-randomised studies, evaluating interventions for street-connected children and young people from birth to 24 years in all contexts. Data collection and analysis involved extracting data on intervention delivery, context, process factors, equity and outcomes, grouped into psychosocial outcomes, risky sexual behaviour or substance use, with meta-analyses conducted for similar outcome measures. Main results included no sufficiently robust evaluations in low-income countries (LMICs), low study quality, variability in measurements, participants classified as drop-in and shelter-based, no studies measuring reintegration primary outcome, none reporting adverse effects, and no consistent results on relevant outcomes across domains. Authors' conclusions emphasized no consistently significant benefit for focused therapeutic interventions compared with standard services, common services not rigorously evaluated, need for more robust evaluation including comparison with no intervention, and additional research needed in LMICs.

filter_requirements: clinical controlled trials (CCTs), controlled before-and-after trials (CBAs) and quasi-randomised trials. We included studies focusing on trauma experiences and mental health impacts but did not evaluate the effectiveness of specific interventions.
