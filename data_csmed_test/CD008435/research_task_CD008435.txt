other_info: Surface Submersion Care (SSC) appears to be effective as measured by composite pain indicators with both physiological and behavioural indicators, and safe for a single painful procedure. Purely behavioural indicators tended to favour SSC but with facial actions there is greater possibility of observers not being blinded. Two studies compared mother-provider to other providers without significant differences. Neonatal pain management has evolved with the introduction of SSC, which is now widely recognized as an effective intervention for reducing pain in both term and preterm infants. New research highlights its efficacy in managing tissue damaging or particularly painful procedures, ensuring safe and reliable outcomes across diverse clinical settings.
